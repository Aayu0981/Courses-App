Harness the potential of artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) has the power to transform our approach to marketing and communication. With its help, marketers can rapidly produce original text and visual content, while automating processes and enhancing efficiency.

On this four-week course from the University of Adelaide, youll learn how you can harness AI for your marketing and communication. From brainstorming ideas to crafting compelling content, youll discover how developments in AI are transforming the industry.

Practise using AI for content creation

While many businesses see boosting visibility as the primary goal of marketing, success cannot be achieved without targeted and impactful content. In Weeks 2 and 3 of the course youll learn how to use AI to generate impactful text- and visual-based content.

Through practical exercises and real-world examples, youll learn how to develop content that resonates with your target audience, increases engagement, and drives meaningful results.

Discover how AI can contribute to measurement, analysis, and planning

Moving beyond content creation, the fourth and final week of the course will look at marketing processes more generally. Youll learn about the role chatbots and AI data tools can play in measurement, analysis, and planning within marketing and communication.

Youll review some existing examples of AI-driven processes, while also discussing potential future developments in the industry.

By the end of the course, youll be prepared to take on the future of AI in marketing and communication.

What will you achieve?

By the end of the course, youll be able to...

  • Identify AI's challenges, opportunities, and potential limitations within marketing and communication.
  • Describe ways of utilising AI effectively for text-based content such as by optimising your approach, engaging in meaningful interactions with prompts, and seamlessly translating AI suggestions into actionable marketing and communication strategies.
  • Experiment with enhancing visual-based content by employing a practical approach, leveraging interactive prompts, and seamlessly incorporating AI-generated suggestions.
  • Evaluate AI prompts and assess how effective they will be in various contexts, transforming suggestions into actionable insights that drive informed decision-making.

Who is the course for?

This is an introductory course aimed both at professionals just starting out in marketing roles and at learners with no prior knowledge of marketing.

Building on two other courses offered by the University of Adelaide (Marketing Essentials and Developing your Marketing Strategy), it will be a useful continuation for learners who have completed these.
